# Sports & Games
*Where the stakes are imaginary and the trash talk is forever.*

1. Golf is the only sport where you drive like a maniac, then spend twenty minutes looking for where you parked.
2. My golf handicap is that I golf.
3. I joined a gym in January and by March I'd achieved my goal weight for my wallet.
4. Fantasy football is a game where you yell at real strangers for ruining your imaginary team.
5. The referee blew the whistle so much I assumed he was training for a kettle championship.
6. Curling is what happens when a chore and a sport love each other very much.
7. Chess players never fear commitment; they've been thinking twelve moves ahead since the first date.
8. My poker face is fine — it's my poker bank account that keeps folding.
9. Participation trophies are great practice for adulthood, where you also get a mug that says you tried.
10. I run a five-minute mile, as long as the mile is measured in car.
11. Esports athletes stretch before games too; it's called reaching for the energy drink.
12. My bowling style is best described as "aggressive donation to the gutter."
13. Soccer is ninety minutes of pretending you weren't offside and forty-five seconds of pretending you're dead.
14. The gym's mirror wall exists so you can watch yourself make bad decisions in real time.
15. I play chess like a pigeon — knock over the pieces, strut around, and declare victory on social media.
16. Marathon training is easy: step one, tell everyone you're marathon training; there is no step two.
17. Hockey players lose teeth so often the tooth fairy has them on a payment plan.
18. Monopoly doesn't ruin friendships; it just runs an audit on them.
19. My fantasy league is so cutthroat we drafted lawyers in the third round.
20. Tennis is just aggressive arguing with a net between you.
21. The hardest part of yoga is pretending the pose isn't your normal reaction to a leg cramp.
22. Baseball is the only job where you can fail seven times out of ten and get a statue.
23. I benched three hundred this week — episodes, but still.
24. Referees don't need glasses; they need witnesses.
25. My chess rating and my credit rating had a race to the bottom, and honestly, photo finish.
26. Dodgeball taught me everything I know about office meetings: keep moving and never volunteer a target.
27. Golf carts exist because someone realized the walk between disappointments was too long.
28. The poker table is the one place where "I have a full house" is bragging instead of a housing crisis.
29. I do CrossFit — I get cross, then I have a fit.
30. Sports parents scream "let the kids play" at anyone who lets the other kids play.
31. My video game backlog is now officially an endurance sport.
32. Curlers are the only athletes whose warm-up is mopping.
33. I asked my personal trainer what I could achieve in a month, and he said "a second month's payment."
34. The fantasy draft is the one day a year I prepare more than I did for my wedding.
35. Boxing is a debate club where every rebuttal is a jab.
36. My bracket was perfect until the games started.
37. Pickleball is tennis for people who want the injuries without the cardio.
38. The scoreboard doesn't lie, which is why nobody in my family will play Scrabble with a dictionary present.
39. I skated on thin ice once — the coach called it my hockey career.
40. Weightlifters don't have trust issues; they just always need a spotter.
41. My kid's soccer league has a snack schedule more strictly enforced than the offside rule.
42. Bowling shoes are the only rental where you pay to look worse.
43. Chess clocks exist because even geniuses need someone to tell them to hurry up.
44. The gym sauna is where cardio goes to be replaced by sweating and gossip.
45. I would play catch with my dad, but he's still going for that gallon of milk — three overtimes now.
46. Fantasy baseball is a spreadsheet with feelings.
47. A hole in one is proof that even golf feels bad for you sometimes.
48. Wrestlers cut weight; poker players cut losses; I cut corners.
49. My esports team has a nutritionist, which is a person who counts your Doritos out loud.
50. The only thing I've ever caught while fishing is a sunburn with a personal best weight.
51. Refs get escorted off the field for their safety; the rest of us just call that "leaving a family game night."
52. Jenga is the only game where the loser also has to clean up the crime scene.
53. I tried spin class, but I was already dizzy from the membership pricing.
54. Free solo climbers and my fantasy team have one thing in common: no backup plan.
55. Tee-ball is baseball with the difficulty settings for parents turned up.
56. My treadmill has a cup holder, which tells you everything about our relationship.
57. Card counters get banned from casinos; I get banned from Uno for the same energy.
58. Basketball players travel constantly and only sometimes get called for it.
59. The clubhouse turn is my favorite part of horse racing because it means the yelling is almost over.
60. I row every morning — with my brother, over the thermostat.
61. Golfers yell "fore," which is generous, because the ball is clearly for whoever it hits.
62. My gym crush is the water fountain.
63. Speed chess is regular chess with the anxiety turned professional.
64. The kiss cam is the only camera at the stadium not reviewing my terrible decisions.
65. Fantasy football commissioners have the power of a judge and the respect of a substitute teacher.
66. I love badminton because "shuttlecock" is the only trash talk you need.
67. Skiing is paying a mountain to file assault charges against you.
68. The eighteenth hole is just the first hole with eighteen excuses behind it.
69. In poker, the tell isn't my face — it's the sound of my last chip whimpering.
70. Youth sports referees make eleven dollars an hour and one hundred enemies a game.
71. Board game night starts with "it's easy, I'll teach you" and ends forty minutes into the rules with no game played.
72. The batting cage is where I go to get personally rejected by a machine.
73. My warm-up routine is telling everyone about my old injury.
74. Rock climbing gyms sell chalk, day passes, and the illusion that your forearms belong to you.
75. Cheerleaders are the only people legally allowed to be that optimistic about my team.
76. Table tennis is regular tennis after taxes.
77. I don't always play Risk, but when I do, someone leaves as an ex-friend and self-declared emperor of Australia.
78. The gym is open twenty-four hours because guilt doesn't sleep.
79. Relay races are just track and field's trust exercises with witnesses.
80. My kid got a participation trophy so big we had to participate in buying a new shelf.
