# Travel & Geography

*Wander often, wonder always, and assume your luggage is doing neither.*

1. My boarding pass says Group 9, which is airline code for "we'll board the plane, then the cargo, then your self-esteem, then you."
2. The airport moving walkway isn't transportation, it's a museum exhibit where the artifact is people standing still on the left.
3. Jet lag is your body politely asking which of the last three Tuesdays it should believe in.
4. I don't have a bucket list, I have a layover list: places I've technically been for forty-five minutes of pure fluorescent despair.
5. The hotel calls it a "city view" because "brick wall with ambition" didn't fit on the website.
6. My suitcase came off the carousel last, dented, and somehow damp, like it had gone on a worse trip than I did.
7. Airport water costs eight dollars because it's been through security and now has trust issues.
8. The middle seat is a philosophical exercise: two armrests, zero rights, infinite elbow diplomacy.
9. Road trip rule: the driver picks the music, the passenger picks the snacks, and the GPS picks the fight.
10. Time zones exist so you can be tired in one place and blamed for it in another.
11. My travel influencer friend calls it "van life," but the van calls it "hostage situation with mood lighting."
12. Every hostel has one guy playing acoustic guitar like the building's lease depends on it.
13. The cruise ship buffet is open twenty-four hours because the sea is vast but the human capacity for shrimp is vaster.
14. I love how the airline says "we know you have a choice" right after proving I absolutely did not.
15. A map is just a piece of paper that's confident, which already makes it more qualified than me.
16. The hotel minibar charges twelve dollars for peanuts because grief is expensive.
17. My luggage got rerouted to a city I've never mentioned aloud, which suggests it's not lost, it's hiding.
18. Turbulence is the sky's way of checking whether you meant that prayer from earlier.
19. Airport sushi is a trust fall with no one standing behind you.
20. I asked the front desk for a wake-up call and they said "you're thirty-five and still using hotel loyalty points as a personality," which was not the kind I meant.
21. The "scenic route" is just the regular route plus your partner saying "we should have turned back there" every nine minutes.
22. Duty-free shops exist so you can panic-buy a liter of gin and a Toblerone the size of a canoe at 6 a.m. and call it savings.
23. My rental car's gas tank was full, my optimism was full, and the return line took both.
24. A layover under an hour isn't a connection, it's a track and field event with a personal item.
25. The hotel pool is open 8 to 8, heated in theory, and guarded by one lifeguard who is also the breakfast chef.
26. Boarding zones are just a caste system with laminated cards.
27. My phone says the flight is delayed, the board says on time, and the gate agent says "we'll know more soon," which is airport for "never."
28. I packed light for once, and my luggage rewarded me by staying behind out of spite for the demotion.
29. The window seat gets the view, the aisle gets the freedom, and the middle gets the character development.
30. Cruise ships are hotels that got tired of waiting for you to come to them and committed to the bit.
31. Every road trip has a moment where the snacks become dinner and nobody acknowledges it.
32. The airport is the only place where a salad costs nineteen dollars and you say "fine" out loud, to no one.
33. My hotel room key stopped working because I put it next to my phone, my other key, my wallet, and apparently my hopes.
34. Google Maps said "you have arrived," and honestly, standing in that parking lot at midnight, I had never felt further from arrival.
35. Jet lag stage one: awake at 4 a.m. Stage two: hungry at 4 a.m. Stage three: emailing your ex at 4 a.m. from a time zone where it's somehow also 4 a.m.
36. The overhead bin space war has no winners, only people holding duffel bags above their heads like offerings to an indifferent god.
37. I trusted a travel blog that said "hidden gem" and found four hundred people photographing the same hidden gem in shifts.
38. My travel pillow has been to eleven countries and supported my neck in zero of them.
39. Hostels charge extra for privacy, which is how I learned privacy is a luxury good and earplugs are currency.
40. The seatback pocket contains a safety card, a magazine from 2019, and the ghost of someone's gum.
41. "Free hotel breakfast" is legally allowed to mean one banana guarding a waffle machine with a line like a DMV.
42. Time zone math on a group chat is how you find out which of your friends would survive a heist and which would call the cops on themselves.
43. The airline app sent me a notification that said "Good news!" and I aged four years before it loaded.
44. My checked bag has more frequent flyer miles than I do and a worse attitude about it.
45. Rest stop bathrooms are graded on a curve, and the curve is the interstate.
46. The tour guide said "this next part isn't for everyone," and then it was stairs. It was just stairs. Four hundred of them.
47. I don't fear flying, I fear the guy who takes his shoes off before the seatbelt sign turns off, like he's moving in.
48. The hotel iron has one setting: subtle new wrinkles.
49. All-inclusive resorts are casinos where the currency is buffet plates and the house always wins your waistband.
50. My GPS said "recalculating" with the exact tone my mother uses when I explain my career.
51. Airport security took my full-size sunscreen but let a man carry on a cello, an emotional support cactus, and a grudge.
52. The road trip playlist is six hours long and somehow we still ended up on the same podcast about a crime nobody solved.
53. "Boutique hotel" means the elevator fits one person or two personalities, not both.
54. I asked for a room away from the elevator and they gave me one away from the plumbing, electricity, and apparently the building.
55. A cruise "day at sea" is when you learn there are only so many times a human can walk the same loop before the loop starts walking you.
56. My luggage was the first one out at baggage claim once, and I photographed it like a solar eclipse.
57. The exit row speech asks if I'm willing and able to assist, and legally I said yes, but spiritually I'm a carry-on.
58. Travel-size toiletries are proof we'll pay double for less if the bottle is cute enough.
59. The hotel hairdryer is attached to the wall, wired to the 1980s, and powered by disappointment.
60. Every airport has a gate that's technically in another county, and it's always yours.
61. I speak four languages: English, bad restaurant French, pointing, and the universal sign for "where is the bathroom, this is urgent."
62. The seat recline debate ends the moment you feel knees in your kidneys and become a philosopher of mutual destruction.
63. "Walking distance" in a travel guide is measured by someone who has never carried a backpack, a grudge, or a child.
64. The airport announcement voice says "final boarding call" like a lullaby and "unattended bags will be destroyed" like a birthday toast.
65. Souvenirs are just receipts you display, proving you paid forty euros to remember a place you complained about the whole time.
66. My hotel had a rooftop bar, which is where I learned "sweeping views" means "you can see the other hotel's better rooftop bar."
67. Long-haul flights serve dinner at 4 p.m., breakfast at midnight, and existential dread on a continuous loop.
68. The rental car agent offered me insurance with the energy of a man who has seen how I merge.
69. A road atlas never once said "recalculating," it just let your family scream at each other with dignity, the old way.
70. Airport chapels exist for people of all faiths, but mostly for people whose connection is in Terminal F and they're in Terminal A.
71. The travel influencer's caption said "lost in wanderlust," but the geotag, the tripod, and the ring light said "found, extremely found."
72. Customs asked if I had anything to declare and I said "emotional baggage," and we both agreed there's no duty on that because it's worthless.
73. My hotel room's "workspace" was a stool facing a mirror, so my office was watching myself give up.
74. The fasten seatbelt sign comes on the moment the drink cart arrives, because physics respects comedy.
75. Trip planning has two phases: the spreadsheet phase and the "we'll figure it out there" phase, and they're the same person one week apart.
76. The moving sidewalk ends with "please watch your step," which is the airport's only sincere sentence.
77. I booked a red-eye to save money, and the money I saved went directly to the coffee industry as a tribute.
78. Every hotel shower has two temperatures: apology and lawsuit.
79. "Continental breakfast" is French for "we found croissants somewhere and it wasn't a bakery."
80. My passport photo looks like I was caught doing something, and after nine hours in economy, the resemblance is finally accurate.
