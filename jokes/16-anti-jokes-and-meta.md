# Anti-Jokes & Meta

*The punchlines have unionized and demand you laugh at literally nothing.*

1. Why did the man bring a ladder to the bar? He was doing repairs on the roof, which is what the ladder was for.
2. A horse walks into a bar. The bartender, following posted health codes, asks it to leave.
3. This joke has no punchline. It has a punchline-shaped hole where confidence used to be.
4. Knock knock. Who's there? A delivery driver, because I ordered a package and that's how doors work.
5. What's brown and sticky? Caramel. I was going to say a stick, but I respect you too much.
6. Why did the chicken cross the road? Foraging behavior. Chickens do not understand roads as a concept.
7. A man tells his doctor, "It hurts when I do this." The doctor bills him for the appointment.
8. I would tell you a joke about the fourth wall, but you're standing right there and it feels rude.
9. What do you call a fish with no eyes? Visually impaired, and please stop pointing it out.
10. My setup and my punchline are in couples therapy. The setup says the punchline never delivers.
11. Two guys walk into a bar. The third one, having watched this twice now, ducks.
12. Why don't scientists trust atoms? Scientists trust atoms. That's the entire basis of chemistry.
13. What's the difference between a joke and a rhetorical question?
14. A priest, a rabbi, and a minister walk into a bar. It's an interfaith conference. The venue has a bar.
15. I told my wife a joke about amnesia. She laughed, then I told it again, artistically.
16. This is joke number sixteen. There is no additional content. Some numbers are just accurate.
17. Why was the math book sad? Books lack the neural architecture required for sadness.
18. What did one wall say to the other wall? Nothing. But the fourth one has been staring at you this whole time.
19. A guy walks into a bar and orders a punchline. The bartender says, "We stopped serving those in the setup."
20. What do you get when you cross a joke with a disclaimer? The following is intended for entertainment purposes only.
21. Why did the comedian retire? The jokes kept writing themselves, and they demanded royalties.
22. Knock knock. Who's there? The setup. The setup who? The setup, refusing to develop further out of spite.
23. What's red and bad for your teeth? A brick. Also cherry soda, but the brick is more immediate.
24. I have a joke about timing.
25. Sorry, that last one was supposed to land later.
26. What do you call a joke that explains itself? This is called a joke that explains itself, because it explains itself.
27. A duck walks into a pharmacy and the pharmacist calls animal control, because ducks cannot legally purchase medication.
28. Why did the scarecrow win an award? Nepotism. His father owned the field.
29. My punchline filed a restraining order against my setup. It has to stay at least three sentences away.
30. What's the best thing about Switzerland? I've never been, so I'd be guessing.
31. A joke walks into a bar. The bartender says, "Is this some kind of guy?"
32. How many surrealists does it take to change a lightbulb? One. Surrealists are perfectly capable of basic home maintenance.
33. This joke contains 40% recycled material from joke 11.
34. Why did the golfer bring two pairs of pants? Weather variability. Golf is an outdoor sport.
35. What did the ocean say to the shore? Nothing. But it did make continuous noise, which the shore misinterpreted as attention.
36. I asked the librarian if the library had any books on irony. She handed me one that was due back yesterday, on time, in perfect condition.
37. Knock knock. Who's there? Nobody. You imagined the knocking. This is a joke about your vigilance.
38. Two antennas met on a roof and did not fall in love, because antennas are equipment.
39. Why is six afraid of seven? Numbers experience no emotion. Seven has been cleared of all charges.
40. What do you call a bear with no teeth? A serious veterinary concern.
41. A man walks into a bar. The bar was set very low. This is that joke.
42. My therapist says I use humor as a defense mechanism. Anyway, that's the joke, please stop advancing.
43. What's orange and sounds like a parrot? A parrot that has been painted orange, in violation of several statutes.
44. Why did the tomato blush? Ripening. It's a lycopene thing. Nobody said anything embarrassing to the tomato.
45. I wrote a joke about procrastination. I'll insert it here eventually.
46. What did the grape do when it got stepped on? Physics. It did physics.
47. A skeleton walks into a bar and immediately collapses, on account of having no muscles, tendons, or central nervous system.
48. Why do we tell jokes in threes? The first establishes, the second reinforces.
49. Waiter, there's a fly in my soup. Sir, this is a joke format from 1950 and the restaurant closed decades ago.
50. What do you call a dog with no legs? By its name, and then you go pick it up, because it's your responsibility.
51. This joke is self-aware. It knows it's number 51. It's fine with it. It didn't want to be in the top 50 anyway.
52. Why don't oysters share? Oysters are filter feeders with no concept of property.
53. I have a joke about a broken pencil, but it works fine, I checked, this whole premise was misinformation.
54. What's the difference between a piano and a fish? A responsible zoologist would never confuse them.
55. A time traveler walks into a bar. A time traveler walks into a bar. He's doing a bit.
56. Why did the bicycle fall over? Insufficient lateral stabilization. Being "two-tired" was never a factor.
57. Knock knock. Who's there? An owl. An owl who? Correct. That is the sound owls make. You've solved it.
58. My joke got rejected by the comedy club. The rejection letter was funnier, so I'm submitting that next.
59. What do you get when you cross the road with a chicken? A traffic incident report.
60. How do you make a tissue dance? You can't. Please stop putting music near the tissues.
61. Why was the belt arrested? It wasn't. Belts are inanimate. The pants were also released without charge.
62. A pun, a play on words, and a double entendre walk into a bar. They're the same person. He's exhausting.
63. What's black and white and red all over? An error in color theory.
64. I told a joke into the void. The void said "heard it," which honestly hurt more than silence.
65. Why did the mushroom get invited to the party? Someone was making risotto.
66. What did the zero say to the eight? Nothing. Numerals are typographic symbols. This has been covered.
67. My punchline showed up late and blamed traffic. There is no traffic between two sentences. I checked.
68. What do you call cheese that isn't yours? Cheese. Ownership doesn't change its name. That's not how nouns work.
69. This number traditionally receives a laugh on arrival. The joke has elected to let the number do the work.
70. A grasshopper walks into a bar. The bartender says nothing, because he does not notice insects at floor level.
71. Why did the picture go to jail? Metaphors were tried as adults in this jurisdiction.
72. What's a pirate's favorite letter? Whichever contains news of his family.
73. I know a great joke about unemployment, but it needs work, and so do I, and that's the actual joke, my life.
74. Knock knock. Who's there? Repetition. Repetition who? Knock knock.
75. Why don't eggs tell jokes? Eggs don't do anything. The bar for egg accomplishment is hatching.
76. A man asks a genie for one joke that always lands. The genie gives him an airport, which is technically where jokes about airline food live.
77. What did the janitor say when he jumped out of the closet? Nothing. He was startled too. Everyone involved apologized.
78. My setup takes three business days to process. Your punchline will arrive in a separate delivery.
79. Why did the coffee file a police report? It didn't. Getting "mugged" is the correct storage condition for coffee.
80. This joke ends abruptly to teach you about impermanence
