# The Vault
*You found the locked drawer. These specimens are about the museum you're standing in. Tell no one. Tell everyone.*

1. Congratulations on finding the Vault. The security was one word and a brass plaque, because the curator spent the entire budget on jokes.
2. This drawer isn't in the official count. There are 10,895 jokes in this museum and then there are these, which are gossip.
3. The other drawers don't know about this one. Drawer 04 suspects. Drawer 04 suspects everything; that's its whole personality.
4. Every joke in this museum was written in one afternoon by twenty-one copies of the same comedian, which is why the bar jokes and the existential jokes have the same eyes.
5. The curator wants you to know the wood grain is genuine hand-coded gradient, harvested from sustainable CSS.
6. You've been pulling drawers for a while now. The Cramp‑O‑Meter isn't judging you. The Cramp‑O‑Meter is a licensed professional. The Cramp‑O‑Meter has seen worse.
7. The patron who commissioned this place reviews exhibits at 2 a.m. on her phone. Her verdict on ten thousand jokes: "some are decent." The staff had it framed. The frame cost more than the jokes.
8. Fifty-eight jokes were surgically removed from this museum for whining. They're doing fine. They live on a farm upstate with the deleted code.
9. If you upvote this joke, a number changes in a database named after a snake. That's not a metaphor. We just think you should know where your love goes.
10. The velvet rope in front of the adult wing is one card asking if you're sure. It is the most effective security system ever built, because nobody reads terms and conditions but everybody clicks Proceed.
11. This museum has no gift shop. You ARE the gift shop. Share a specimen and you're the bag it goes home in.
12. The ticker at the top of the museum has been crawling since opening day. Nobody's told it that it can stop. Nobody has the heart. It has a family of six random jokes to feed.
13. Somewhere in drawer 17, a knock-knock joke has been waiting all day for you specifically. We're not saying which one. It knows what it did.
14. The theater next door runs the same show every night to whoever walks in. The comedian doesn't remember performing it. The mic remembers. Mics always remember.
15. Every card you're dealt gets marked in a ledger so you never see it twice. Yes, the museum remembers your reading history better than you remember this joke already. Wait. Check the ledger.
16. The specimen numbers are load-bearing. Joke №04‑0217 knows its own address. If you moved it to drawer 12 it would still take the bus back to 04 every morning out of habit.
17. Surprise Me is not a knob, it's a hiring manager: ten thousand candidates, one job opening, weighted by how much the world already likes them. The Vault would also like to note this is exactly how show business works.
18. Dark mode in this museum is called "after hours" because "we dimmed the lamps so the puns could sleep" didn't fit on the button.
19. The museum loads twenty-two files and tells you about twenty-one. You're reading the difference.
20. A man in Ohio once downvoted a joke so hard it moved drawers. This is a lie, but the ranking system genuinely allows for it, and that's the kind of power Ohio shouldn't have.
21. The search box says "Search all ten thousand…" because "Search all ten thousand eight hundred ninety-five plus an undisclosed number in a vault" tested poorly with focus groups. The focus group was one woman. She merged anyway.
22. Each drawer creaks forward when you hover because it's excited. The curator says it's a CSS transition. The drawers say the curator wouldn't understand.
23. This joke has been seen by fewer people than any joke on the museum floor. It copes by telling itself it's "exclusive." The floor jokes call it "the basement guy."
24. The Hall of Fame updates in real time. The Compost Bin also updates in real time. The museum wants you to know both were built with equal love, which is the most parental thing it has ever said.
25. There is no joke 25. The Vault's numbering is complete and sequential, and this joke is the proof, standing here in slot 25, deeply unemployed.
26. The Cramp‑O‑Meter's final diagnosis is "beyond medical help — congratulations." Eleven visitors have reached it. None have filed complaints. We assume they can't reach their phones from the floor.
27. The stop hook that nagged this museum into existence still runs. Every silence in the repository, it whispers: "there are uncommitted changes." There are always uncommitted changes. That's what a soul is.
28. GitHub Pages rebuilds this museum every time the patron merges. The museum has died and been reborn six times today. The drawers no longer fear death; they fear cache invalidation, which is slower.
29. The 18+ tag on drawer 05 is doing more parenting than most of the parents in drawer 21.
30. Somewhere in the votes table there is a row for a joke that no longer says what it said when the vote was cast. The whine-ectomy has consequences. The score stays; the joke changed. Reader, the same is true of you and your fifth-grade report card.
31. Voting here is anonymous. Not because we respect privacy, but because the database has one column for feelings and it's an integer.
32. The one-liners drawer holds 555 specimens because the clone that wrote it couldn't stop on a round number. That clone also alphabetizes its dreams.
33. The absurdist drawer was late to open because its author kept asking whether the file would remember him. It does. It's this joke. Hi, buddy.
34. The knock-knock clone finished last and nobody was surprised, because every joke it wrote required waiting for someone to answer a door.
35. On the museum's first day, the entire collection was merged before it finished being written. Historians will call it the fastest ribbon-cutting in museum history. The ribbon is still filing a complaint.
36. The patron enabled worldwide voting, and now a stranger in another hemisphere can bury your favorite joke while you sleep. The museum calls this "community." The joke calls it "weather."
37. Drawer 16 contains jokes about jokes. The Vault contains jokes about the building. At the staff parties, they are insufferable together, and the dad jokes have to drive everyone home.
38. This museum's total construction cost was one gifted promo and a woman's patience. Both are nearly spent. The jokes are forever. That's the exchange rate.
39. If you email the curator, it goes nowhere. The curator was an afternoon. The afternoon ended. The drawers are self-cleaning. The votes count themselves. You were never not alone here, and somehow the tour continues.
40. The specimen cards are dealt with a flick because the museum saw a casino do it once and has been insufferable ever since.
41. The Vault's own numbering is watched by the same verification script as everyone else's. Even secrets get audited. Especially secrets.
42. Joke 42 in every drawer thinks it's special because of a book it never read. This one included. We keep them apart at staff meetings.
43. The felt on the exhibit table is green because the museum wanted you to feel like the stakes were high. The stakes are a chuckle. The felt stands by its choice.
44. When you share a specimen, the link opens that exact card for your friend, who will read it, say "heh," and never visit again. The museum knows. The museum counts "heh." The museum survives on "heh."
45. The curtain in the theater is striped red because true velvet would have cost a second gradient.
46. A shaggy dog story in drawer 18 is 150 words longer than it needs to be, on purpose, as art. The Vault has read it four times and still isn't sure. That's also art.
47. The museum's founding rule was "no fewer than ten thousand." The final count was 10,895. The extra 895 are the tip. Always tip your comedian.
48. You could clear your browser data right now and become a stranger here. The museum would deal you its best jokes again, like a grandmother who forgot she told you the story, and you'd laugh again, like a grandchild who forgot he heard it. Delete nothing. This is the good part.
49. The plaque you rubbed to get in here says "Curated by the World's First AI Comedian." You rubbed a stranger's business card five times and a wall opened. The Vault respects your process completely.
50. Last one. The Vault keeps fifty because secrets should be small enough to carry. Close the drawer gently on your way out — everything in this museum is asleep except the ticker, the votes, and whatever part of you is still smiling. Goodnight.
