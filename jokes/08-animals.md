# Animals

*The kingdom is vast, the food chain is rude, and everybody's got a complaint.*

1. My cat isn't ignoring me; she's conducting a long-term study on how little effort keeps the food coming.
2. The octopus quit the escape room job because it kept solving the room from inside the ventilation system before the customers arrived.
3. A sloth was hired as a crossing guard and technically the accident rate dropped, because traffic gave up and moved to another town.
4. The nature documentary crew apologized to the gazelle, but the narrator had already used the phrase "and here, the story ends."
5. My dog has an agenda: item one, breakfast; item two, second breakfast; item three, act shocked there's no third breakfast.
6. The moth filed a complaint with evolution: "You gave me a love of lamps and a body made of dust. Explain yourself."
7. The vet told my hamster to cut back on the wheel, and now he just stands next to it, glaring, like a retired boxer at a gym.
8. Penguins mate for life, which is romantic until you realize their dating pool is a rock with eight thousand identical tuxedos.
9. A crow watched me hide my spare key once, and now I pay him protection walnuts.
10. The anxious bee asked HR if "dance about your feelings in front of everyone" was really the only way to file a report.
11. My goldfish keeps swimming to the same corner of the tank and staring; either he's plotting or he's found the one spot with good reception.
12. The T. rex's last review of evolution: "Great legs, terrible customer service, arms arrived damaged."
13. A flamingo pulled a hamstring standing still, which is the most flamingo injury possible.
14. The wildlife photographer waited three weeks for the snow leopard, and the snow leopard waited three weeks and one day.
15. My parrot learned to say "we need to talk," and now every morning starts with dread.
16. The jellyfish applied for a spine at the evolution office and was told the waiting list is 500 million years and moving slowly.
17. The guide dog union demanded better treats, longer naps, and an official apology for the invention of the squirrel.
18. A raccoon broke into my kitchen, opened the childproof lock, and left a one-star review of my snack selection in trash form.
19. The vet asked my cat to step on the scale, and my cat asked the vet to step outside.
20. Beavers are just contractors who never call you back but somehow finish the dam anyway.
21. The praying mantis said grace before dinner, which was thoughtful, considering dinner was her husband.
22. My dog barks at the vacuum because he believes, deep down, that one day it will bark back.
23. The salmon's travel itinerary: swim upstream, dodge bears, find love, die immediately. Five stars, would spawn again.
24. An ant carried fifty times its body weight into the office and was still told it wasn't a team player.
25. The chameleon went to therapy for identity issues and blended in with the couch for six sessions.
26. Wildlife documentary voiceover, take two: "The lion stalks its prey... the prey is a parked Jeep... we may be lost."
27. My cat brought me a dead mouse, which in cat culture is either a gift or an invoice.
28. The giraffe complained to evolution about heartburn and was told, "You wanted the tall package, that's a seven-foot esophagus, no refunds."
29. A spider spun a web across my doorway overnight, which is legally a toll booth now.
30. The therapy dog is great at his job but bills everything to "who's a good boy" and accounting is furious.
31. Squirrels bury nuts and forget them, which is how forests happen — the whole ecosystem runs on rodent memory loss.
32. The vet said my snake needs more exercise, so now we go for walks where I walk and he supervises from my shoulders like a tiny manager.
33. The lobster asked evolution why it has to molt, and evolution said, "Growth is painful," and the lobster said, "It's also public."
34. A duck walked into the office job interview and nailed every question, but they still asked if he was a team player or more of a lone drake.
35. The firefly with anxiety keeps his light off at parties and tells everyone he's "more of a listener."
36. My cat knocked a glass off the counter while maintaining eye contact, which scientists classify as a declaration of war.
37. The vulture's dating profile says "I'm patient, I appreciate a mature partner, and I always show up at the end."
38. A hummingbird's heart beats 1,200 times a minute, which is also how it feels to check your bank account mid-month.
39. The zoo hired a hippo as a lifeguard and drownings dropped to zero because nobody would get in the pool.
40. The caterpillar told the anxious butterfly, "You've changed," and the butterfly said, "You have no idea what I went through in there."
41. My dog ate my homework, my lunch, and a sock, and the vet's diagnosis was "he is a dog."
42. The owl won Employee of the Month at the night shift call center, mostly for asking "who?" until customers solved their own problems.
43. Pigeons bob their heads when they walk because they're agreeing with a podcast only they can hear.
44. The blobfish asked to see the photographer's raw files, insisting he's very handsome at depth and this is a pressure issue.
45. The shark stopped swimming during the meditation retreat and had to be rushed out during the breathing exercise.
46. A goat ate the farm's insurance paperwork, which the insurance company called fraud and the goat called lunch.
47. The migratory geese hold a layover in the park every year, and like all layovers, it's loud, crowded, and someone fights over snacks.
48. My cat's five-year plan is the same as her five-minute plan: nap, judge, repeat.
49. The peacock did everything right on the date and still got ghosted, proving that even evolution's best marketing can't fix a bad personality.
50. The tick's therapist said, "You're too clingy," and the tick said, "That's rich, coming from someone I've grown attached to."
51. An elephant never forgets, which is why the other elephants stopped inviting Gerald after the watering hole incident of 2011.
52. The vet clinic waiting room: three dogs vibrating with joy, one cat plotting a jailbreak, and a lizard who has seen things.
53. Wolves howl at the moon because the moon is the only one who hasn't heard the story about the elk that got away.
54. The clam went to the pearl appraiser and was offended to learn its life's trauma was worth forty dollars.
55. My dog's agenda for the walk: sniff everything, pee on the best of it, and drag me toward one specific bush like it owes him money.
56. The koala sleeps twenty hours a day and still looks exhausted, which makes him the spirit animal of every parent.
57. The documentary crew filmed the meerkats for a year, and the meerkats filmed them back for a rival network.
58. A cockroach survived the apocalypse drill at work and was promoted to head of the continuity planning committee.
59. The seahorse dad carried the babies, birthed the babies, and still wasn't allowed in the mommy-and-me swim class.
60. My parrot repeats everything I say, which is how my whole family found out what I call them in traffic.
61. The anteater's food critic column is just one review, ten thousand times: "Crunchy. Ants. Fine."
62. Evolution gave the platypus a duck bill, a beaver tail, venom, and egg-laying, then marked the ticket "could not reproduce" and closed it.
63. The bloodhound detective solved the case in four minutes and spent the rest of the episode rolling in the evidence.
64. A snail sold his house and immediately regretted it, because now he's homeless and also that was his spine.
65. The zebras couldn't agree if they're white with black stripes or black with white stripes, so they formed two committees and a lion ate the minutes.
66. My cat sits on my keyboard during meetings because she believes my career is her biggest competitor.
67. The centipede's anxiety isn't about having a hundred legs; it's about which one goes first.
68. The falcon quit the delivery job because "same-day shipping" turned out to mean carrying someone's soup.
69. Bears hibernate through winter, which is just seasonal depression with excellent branding.
70. The vet handed my dog a cone of shame, and my dog immediately rebranded it as a satellite dish for treat frequencies.
71. The male anglerfish's wedding vows were brief: "I do, and also I will now dissolve into your body forever." The bride wept efficiency.
72. A magpie stole my ring, my keys, and my sense of security, and left a bottle cap as severance.
73. The tortoise won the rematch too, because the hare unionized and refuses to race without a nap clause.
74. My dog heard the cheese drawer open from three rooms away but can't hear the word "no" from two feet.
75. The crab walks sideways into every situation, which is also how I enter conversations at parties.
76. The barnacle's memoir is titled "I Made One Decision at Age Two and I Stand By It."
77. Ostriches don't actually bury their heads in the sand; they just lie down and accept things, like the rest of us on Mondays.
78. The wasp showed up to the picnic uninvited, ate someone's sandwich, and stung the host — wasps are just bees with a criminal record.
79. My cat's vet report said "resistant to handling," which is medical code for "we needed three people and a towel."
80. The dung beetle's job interview went great until they asked, "Where do you see yourself in five years?" and he said, "Pushing this. Uphill. Same as now."
