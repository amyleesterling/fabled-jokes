# Dark Humor

*Warning: these jokes go to dark places and do not offer to pay for gas — read at your own risk.*

1. My therapist told me to write letters to the people I hate and then burn them. I did, but now I don't know what to do with all these letters.
2. Grandpa always said the key to a long life is moderation. He was moderately alive until 61.
3. I told the funeral director I wanted something simple for Dad. He said, "Cremation?" I said, "No, just tell people he moved."
4. The doctor gave me six months to live, so I shot him. The judge gave me twenty-five years. Problem solved.
5. My family has a proud tradition of dying right before payday. We call it fiscal responsibility.
6. I asked my grandmother what it was like being married for sixty years. She said, "Quieter now."
7. My will just says "Good luck proving I owed you anything."
8. Dad's last words were "Be careful with the ladder." Great advice. Terrible timing.
9. The orphanage had a Bring Your Dad To School Day. Attendance was perfect. Dads, less so.
10. I donated my body to science. Science sent it back with a note that said "We have standards."
11. My grief counselor died. He was so good at his job I honestly don't care.
12. They say you die twice: once when you stop breathing, and again the last time someone says your name. So my plan is to owe a lot of people money.
13. I bought a burial plot with a great view. Of the highway. Mom always liked watching traffic she wasn't stuck in.
14. My uncle's dying wish was to have his ashes spread across his favorite bar. Health code says we have until Thursday to sweep him up.
15. The life insurance salesman asked if I had any dangerous hobbies. I said, "Marriage." He wrote down "skydiving" out of kindness.
16. I finally finished my bucket list. Turns out "kick it" was the last item all along.
17. Mom said I'd be late to my own funeral. Jokes on her — I was early to hers.
18. My doctor said, "Whatever you're doing, keep doing it." Then he looked at my chart again and said, "Wait. Stop immediately."
19. I like to think of my student loans as a hostage situation where I'm both the hostage and the ransom.
20. The nursing home has a suggestion box. It's mostly full of escape plans.
21. My grandfather has the heart of a lion and a lifetime ban from the zoo, and the two facts are related.
22. I wrote "get buried in something comfortable" in my will, so they're putting me in debt, same as I lived.
23. We told Grandma the tunnel with the light at the end was a train. She's too stubborn to be hit by anything, so she came back.
24. My father's ghost keeps haunting me, which is funny because he never visited when it was convenient for either of us.
25. I asked the palliative nurse how long Dad had. She said, "Days." Dad said, "Rude. I can hear you." She said, "Hours, then."
26. The cemetery raised its prices. People are dying to get in, but honestly, at these rates, I'd rather live.
27. My aunt beat cancer twice. Cancer got smart the third time and brought a bus.
28. I keep my enemies close and my emergency contacts closer, because those lists overlap more than you'd think.
29. My last words are going to be "I hid a million dollars in the—" just to keep everyone busy.
30. The optimist says the glass is half full. The pessimist says it's half empty. The coroner says it's what killed him either way.
31. Dad taught me to swim the old-fashioned way: he threw me in the lake. Learning to swim was easy. Trusting him again took decades.
32. I planted a tree over my dog's grave. Now he chases squirrels for eternity, and honestly the squirrels had it coming.
33. My family plays a fun game at funerals called "Who's Next?" Grandpa was undefeated until he won.
34. The hospital billed me for the ambulance ride I don't remember. I told them I want the memory or a refund.
35. I told my kids the smoke detector chirping means the house loves them. Cheaper than batteries, riskier than truth.
36. My grandfather's last words were "Stop shaking the ladder, you idiots." He was a motivational speaker to the end.
37. Life is like a candle: brief, flickering, and someone always blows it out right when the party gets good.
38. They asked what I wanted on my tombstone. I said, "Pepperoni." Read the room, sure, but also read the menu.
39. I've started telling people I'm not lazy, I'm conserving energy for the crisis. The crisis is Tuesday.
40. The support group for procrastinators finally met. At the funeral of the founder. As scheduled.
41. My cat brought me a dead bird as a gift, which is still more thoughtful than anything my ex ever gave me.
42. Statistically, one in three of my friends will die of heart disease, which is why I only keep two friends.
43. The doctor said my blood type was rare. Then he smiled like a man planning a heist.
44. My grandmother knitted me a sweater from beyond the grave. Which is to say, it arrived after she died and it fits like a curse.
45. I asked my dad for his blessing. He gave me his cholesterol instead. Family is about inheritance.
46. My hometown is so small the obituary section is just called "News."
47. My therapist says I use humor to avoid dealing with my problems. Anyway, two skeletons walk into a bar.
48. The airline lost my luggage and my will to live, but only offered compensation for one of them.
49. Grandpa survived two wars, three heart attacks, and one marriage. He says the marriage was the closest call.
50. I told my son the goldfish went to live on a farm. He asked why the farm was in the toilet. Kid's going to be a detective or a coroner.
51. Every family photo is just a countdown timer with smiles.
52. My life coach quit. Not the job. Just generally.
53. The funeral home offers a loyalty card. Five burials and the sixth is free. My family is two punches away and it's been a bad year.
54. I asked the fortune teller how I'd die. She looked at my palm and said, "Slowly, in meetings."
55. My blood pressure is so high my doctor doesn't give me advice anymore, just eye contact.
56. The escape room gave up on us and unlocked the door after two hours. It's the closest my marriage counselor ever came to a breakthrough.
57. We buried Grandpa with his phone in case of a mistake. Now the family group chat has a typing indicator nobody talks about.
58. My New Year's resolution was to be more spontaneous. So I bought a cemetery plot on impulse.
59. My insurance covers acts of God but not acts of my brother-in-law, which is a shame because he's more consistent.
60. I finally found something that gets me out of bed in the morning: the smoke.
61. The obituary said Grandpa "passed peacefully surrounded by family," which is technically true if you count us fighting over the house in the hallway.
62. My inner child wants ice cream. My inner adult wants sleep. My inner corpse is just glad to be included in the conversation.
63. The doctor asked if I smoke. I said only when the toaster's involved.
64. My dog and I have an agreement: I pretend I'm walking him for his health, he pretends he doesn't smell death on the vet's letter.
65. I keep a diary so that after I die, someone will finally read something I wrote.
66. The gym offered a lifetime membership discount. Given my lifestyle, that's barely a subscription.
67. My grandmother's secret recipe called for a pinch of salt and forty years of quiet resentment.
68. Every time someone says "life is short," a life insurance actuary somewhere nods and adjusts my premium.
69. My father said he'd be at my graduation over his dead body. Points for accuracy.
70. My town's suicide hotline merged with the pizza place. Now when you call, either way, someone shows up in thirty minutes who's paid to care.
71. The haunted house refunded us because Grandma walked through it critiquing the decor. The ghosts filed a complaint.
72. I told my doctor I wanted a second opinion. He said, "Okay. You're also ugly."
73. The hardest part of writing my will was finding a lawyer who could notarize a middle finger.
74. My family tree has a lot of dead ends, and one of them keeps texting me on holidays.
75. I asked the mortician if he liked his job. He said the customers never complain, and the ones that would are his best advertising.
76. The retirement seminar was standing room only because the chairs were repossessed. It set the tone honestly.
77. Grandma outlived three husbands, and only one of those was an accident, which is a better ratio than the prosecutor implied.
78. My sleep tracker says I achieved "deep rest" for eight hours. My smoke alarm has a different word for it.
79. The kids asked where Grandpa went. We said "a better place," and they asked if it had Wi-Fi, and honestly, if it does, he's not in the better place.
80. I put "died doing what he loved" on my uncle's headstone. He loved ignoring safety warnings.
