# Story Jokes & Shaggy Dogs
*Long walks, short drops: tales that take the scenic route to the punchline.*

1. A man finds a genie in a thrift-store teapot. The genie yawns and says, "Budget genie. One wish, no refunds, and I'm dyslexic." The man thinks carefully and says, "I want a huge house full of money." The genie snaps his fingers, and the man spends the rest of his life in an enormous mouse full of honey. The mouse, to his credit, never once complains about the tenant, though he does keep asking the man to stop calling the situation "sticky."
2. A farmer buys a rooster advertised as "the hardest worker in three counties." First morning, the rooster fertilizes the hens, then the ducks, then the geese, then starts eyeing the tractor. The farmer says, "Slow down, son, you'll kill yourself." A week later the farmer finds the rooster flat on his back in the yard, buzzards circling overhead. "I warned you," says the farmer. The rooster opens one eye and whispers, "Quiet, fool, they're about to land."
3. A priest, a rabbi, and a lawyer are stranded on a desert island. The priest prays for rescue, the rabbi builds a signal fire, and the lawyer draws a line in the sand and sues the ocean for encroachment. A week later a coast guard ship arrives. "We saw the fire?" asks the rabbi. "Heard my prayers?" asks the priest. "No," says the captain, "we got served."
4. A talking dog walks into a talent agency. The agent sighs, "Let me guess, you do impressions." The dog says, "No, I write grant proposals." The agent blinks. "Prove it." The dog pulls out a laptop and within an hour secures $2 million in arts funding for the agency. The agent, stunned, asks how a dog learned to do that. The dog shrugs. "Fifteen years of being told 'sit' and 'stay' and I figured out that's the whole job."
5. A man dies and finds two doors in the afterlife: one marked HEAVEN and one marked LECTURE ABOUT HEAVEN. The line for the lecture is around the block. He asks an angel why. The angel sighs, "Academics."
6. A woman brings her parrot to a vet and says, "He won't stop swearing." The vet suggests the freezer trick: thirty seconds in the cold to shock him quiet. She tries it. The parrot comes out shivering and polite as a butler. "Madam, I deeply apologize for my language. I shall reform entirely." Then, after a pause: "One question. What did the chicken do?"
7. A genie offers a fisherman three wishes but warns that his ex-wife's new husband gets double whatever he wishes. The fisherman wishes for a mansion; the other guy gets two. He wishes for a million dollars; the other guy gets two million. For his last wish, the fisherman hands the genie an oar and says, "Beat me half to death."
8. Three engineers argue about God's profession. The mechanical engineer says, "Look at the joints and levers of the body — clearly a mechanical engineer." The electrical engineer says, "The nervous system runs on current — obviously electrical." The civil engineer shakes his head. "God's a civil engineer. Who else would run a sewage line through a recreational area?"
9. A man tells his doctor, "Everywhere I touch, it hurts. My knee, my forehead, my elbow — agony." The doctor runs every test in the building, then sits him down. "I have good news and bad news. The good news is it's completely curable." The man exhales. "And the bad news?" "Your finger's broken."
10. A vampire walks into a blood bank and asks for a job application. The manager says, "Sir, this seems like an obvious conflict of interest." The vampire says, "On the contrary. Who's more motivated to prevent shrinkage than a man who considers the inventory dinner?" He gets the job, and shrinkage drops to zero, because every would-be thief has to get past an interview that begins, "So. You like blood."
11. A farmer's mule stops working, so he calls a mule whisperer. The whisperer picks up a two-by-four and cracks the mule across the flank. "I thought you whispered to them!" the farmer yells. The whisperer says, "I do. But first I have to get a dial tone."
12. Two nuns are driving through Transylvania when a vampire lands on their hood. "Show him your cross!" shouts the first nun. The second nun rolls down the window and screams, "GET OFF THE GODDAMN CAR!" The vampire flees. The first nun stares. The second shrugs, "You said show him I'm cross."
13. A man prays every day for twenty years: "Lord, let me win the lottery." Finally the clouds part and a thunderous voice says, "MY SON. MEET ME HALFWAY. GOOGLE WHICH DAYS THEY DO THE DRAWINGS."
14. A snail gets mugged by two turtles. At the police station, the officer asks him to describe the attackers. The snail rubs his head and says, "Honestly, officer, I can't. It all happened so fast."
15. A CEO, a union rep, and an intern sit at a table with a plate of twelve cookies. The CEO takes eleven, leans over to the intern, and whispers, "Careful, that union guy wants a piece of your cookie." The intern nods, eats his cookie in one bite, and then eats the CEO's eleven while he's giving a speech about scarcity. Moral: never explain the game to someone hungrier than you.
16. A ghost floats into a bar at closing time. The bartender says, "Sorry, we don't serve spirits after midnight." The ghost sighs, "That's fine, I'm actually here about the ad for a regular. It says the position's been open since 1987." The bartender nods at a barstool with a rope around it. "Died in that seat, still hasn't paid his tab. You cover his balance, the stool's yours." The ghost checks the tab, whistles, and passes on through the wall. Some debts even eternity won't cosign.
17. A koala falls out of a tree, lands on a jogger, and knocks him flat. The koala checks the man's pulse, drags him to a bench, calls emergency services, and waits with him until the ambulance arrives. The paramedic says, "That's remarkable for a koala." The koala says, "I'm required to. It's koalafied immunity, but only if I render aid."
18. A man's wife sends him to the store: "Get a gallon of milk, and if they have eggs, get six." He comes home with six gallons of milk. She stares. "Why six gallons?" He says, "They had eggs." She starts to yell, then stops, because she married a programmer knowing full well what he was.
19. A lawyer dies and arrives at the pearly gates to a full brass band and confetti. "What's the occasion?" he asks. Saint Peter checks his ledger. "According to your billable hours, you're 193 years old."
20. Two hunters get lost in the woods. One remembers, "If you're lost, fire three shots in the air and someone will find you." They fire three shots. Nothing. Three more. Nothing. The first hunter loads again and his buddy grabs his arm: "Go easy — we're almost out of arrows."
21. A grasshopper hops into a bar. The bartender says, "Hey, we've got a drink named after you!" The grasshopper says, "You've got a drink named Kevin?" The bartender pauses, then quietly renames the menu, because honestly, Kevin has done more for this bar than mint ever did.
22. God calls a staff meeting of angels and says, "I'm giving humans free will." Gabriel raises a hand. "Sir, projections show they'll use it mostly for arguing on tablets." God says, "Stone tablets?" Gabriel checks his notes. "Eventually, sir, yes."
23. A pirate walks into a bar with a ship's wheel bolted to the front of his trousers. The bartender says, "You know you've got a wheel down there?" The pirate nods grimly. "Aye. It's drivin' me nuts, but the chiropractor says it's the only thing keepin' me course true."
24. A man buys a robot that slaps people who lie. He brings it to dinner. His son says, "I was at the library." Slap. "Fine, I was at a friend's watching movies." Slap. "Okay, movies I shouldn't watch!" The dad laughs, "Son, at your age I'd never—" Slap. The mom cackles, "Well, he's definitely your son—" and the robot dives across the table.
25. A tortoise files a police report: mugged by a gang of snails. Weeks later, an officer calls with news — they caught one. "Great!" says the tortoise. "Did he confess?" "He tried to," says the officer, "but honestly, we're still on the first syllable."
26. A monastery's abbot notices an error in a hand-copied manuscript, so he descends to the vault to check the original. Hours pass. The monks find him weeping over a crumbling scroll. "Father, what's wrong?" He holds up the parchment with shaking hands. "The word was 'celebrate.'"
27. A duck walks into a pharmacy and asks for lip balm. "Cash or card?" asks the clerk. The duck says, "Just put it on my bill." The clerk, who has worked retail for eleven years and has heard everything, doesn't even smile — she just staples the receipt to an invoice marked ACCOUNTS PAYABLE: WATERFOWL, because this duck has a tab, this duck always has a tab, and one day the duck will pay, and on that day she can finally retire.
28. Sherlock Holmes and Watson go camping. At 3 a.m., Holmes nudges Watson awake. "Look up. What do you deduce?" Watson says, "Millions of stars; if even a few have planets, likely some support life." Holmes sighs, "Watson, you spectacular idiot. Someone has stolen our tent."
29. A dog walks into a telegram office and dictates: "Woof woof. Woof woof woof. Woof woof." The clerk says, "That's only seven words — you can add one more woof for free." The dog stares at him. "But then it wouldn't make any sense."
30. An old man on his deathbed smells his favorite cookies baking. He crawls downstairs, reaches a trembling hand toward the cooling rack, and his wife smacks it with a spatula. "Don't touch those," she says. "They're for the funeral."
31. A genie grants a wish to a man who says, "I want a highway from here to Hawaii." The genie winces. "The pylons, the engineering, the environmental review — pick something else." The man says, "Fine. Make me understand my wife." The genie rubs his temples. "Two lanes or four?"
32. Three mice are bragging in a bar. First mouse: "I bench-press mousetraps for fun." Second mouse: "I snort rat poison as a pre-workout." The third mouse finishes his drink and heads for the door. "Where are you going?" they ask. "Home," he says. "It's my night to interrogate the cat."
33. A new priest is so nervous at his first mass that the monsignor suggests a shot of vodka in his water glass. Afterward, he finds a note in the sacristy: "Sipping, not gulping. There are ten commandments, not twelve. There are twelve disciples, not ten. And we do not refer to the crucifixion as 'when Jesus got absolutely nailed.'"
34. A woman calls tech support: "My computer says it can't find the printer." The technician walks her through everything, no luck. Finally he asks her to check the cable. Long pause. "Sir," she says, "I don't know how to tell you this, but the printer is in a different house. My ex took it." The technician says, "Ma'am, that's actually the one problem in this job I know how to fix: the computer's right. Stop looking for the printer."
35. A bear walks into a diner and says, "I'll have the... ... ... pancakes." The waitress asks, "Why the big pause?" The bear holds them up. "Genetics. But I'm asking about pancakes."
36. A man joins a silent monastery where he's allowed two words every ten years. After ten years he says, "Bed hard." Ten more: "Food cold." Ten more: "I quit." The abbot nods, "Frankly, I'm not surprised. You've done nothing but complain since you got here."
37. An octopus walks into a bar and bets he can play any instrument in the house. He nails the guitar, kills on piano, shreds the fiddle. Then someone hands him bagpipes. The octopus turns it over, squints, and says, "Give me a minute — as soon as I get its pajamas off, we can play."
38. A politician dies and gets a trial day in both heaven and hell. Hell turns out to be a golf resort with lobster buffets and all his old friends. Heaven is pleasant but quiet. He chooses hell — and lands in a burning wasteland of ash. "Where's the resort?" he screams. The devil shrugs, "Yesterday we were campaigning. Today you voted."
39. A wife texts her husband, "Windows frozen, won't open." He texts back, "Pour warm water around the edges and tap gently with a hammer." Ten minutes later: "Okay, that's done. Now the laptop won't turn on at all."
40. A scarecrow gets promoted to head of farm security. At his retirement party decades later, the farmer gives a toast: "He was outstanding in his field — but more importantly, when the crows finally unionized, he was the only one they'd negotiate with. Turns out the thing they wanted all along was for someone to just stand there and listen."
41. A man rubs a lamp and out pops a genie the size of a thumb. "Before you ask," says the genie, "yes, the wishes are proportional." The man wishes for wealth and gets forty dollars. He wishes for fame and gets recognized once at a gas station. For his third wish he says, "Make me tiny too." Now he's the richest, most famous man in a world forty dollars deep, and honestly he's never been happier.
42. A farmer enters his pig in the county fair. The judge asks, "Why does this pig have a wooden leg?" The farmer says, "This pig pulled my son from a pond, woke us during a fire, and fought off a coyote." The judge says, "That doesn't explain the leg." The farmer looks at him like he's simple. "Mister, a pig like that, you don't eat all at once."
43. A priest, an imam, and an atheist walk out of a burning building carrying the same painting. The reporter asks the priest why he saved it: "It shows God's creation." The imam: "It shows the divine order." The atheist: "It shows the fire exit map, you absolute lunatics, and I'm going back in to hang it where people can see it."
44. A talking dog runs for city council. At the debate, his opponent sneers, "My rival licks himself in public." The dog steps to the microphone and says, "I have never once pretended to be something I'm not, and my opponent has three golf memberships he reports as charity." The dog wins in a landslide, mostly on the strength of undecided voters who said, quote, "At least when he begs, he's honest about it."
45. A man asks a librarian, "Do you have any books on turtles?" She says, "Hardback?" He says, "Yeah, with little heads." She hires him on the spot for the children's section, on the grounds that he is already fluent in the language.
46. A skeleton walks into a doctor's office. The doctor looks up and says, "Now you come in? NOW?" The skeleton shrugs, which takes a while. "The parking here is terrible."
47. Two cows are standing in a field. One says, "Aren't you worried about that mad cow disease going around?" The other says, "Why would I be? I'm a helicopter." The first cow nods slowly and takes one very quiet step to the left, because the guidance counselor said not to argue with Steve anymore.
48. A magician's rabbit walks into a pawn shop with a top hat. "How much for this?" The clerk examines it. "Ten bucks." The rabbit says, "It's a magic hat. Reach inside." The clerk reaches in and pulls out a magician. The magician looks around wildly and says, "How long was I in there?" The rabbit counts the ten dollars and says, "Long enough to learn who does the actual work in this act."
49. A man dies and is offered reincarnation as anything he wants. "Something where I lie around all day and people bring me food and everyone loves me unconditionally." The angel stamps his form. He wakes up as a golden retriever in a family of six. Every day is the best day of his life. There's no punchline. Some guys just win.
50. A lawyer's car breaks down outside a farm. The farmer offers to help, and while they work, the farmer's dog keeps staring at the lawyer. "Your dog's making me nervous," the lawyer says. "Don't mind him," says the farmer, "he just ain't never seen anybody put their whole arm in an engine to retrieve a business card they threw in on purpose."
51. A woman finds a frog in the woods that says, "Kiss me and I'll become a brilliant investment banker!" She puts him in her pocket. The frog protests, "Didn't you hear me?" She says, "I heard you. But a talking frog is one of one, and a banker who's honest about what he'll become is a short."
52. A drill sergeant screams at a recruit, "I didn't see you at camouflage training this morning!" The recruit stands very still and says, "Thank you, sir."
53. A man walks into a bar with a slab of asphalt under one arm and says, "A beer for me, and one for the road." The bartender serves them both, because the last guy who mocked the asphalt got flattened, and the case is technically still open.
54. A rabbi and a priest are at a boxing match. Before the bell, one fighter crosses himself. The priest's friend the rabbi asks, "What does that mean?" The priest watches the fighter wobble to the center of the ring and says, "Not a damn thing if he can't throw a left."
55. A genie tells a woman, "Three wishes, but every wish costs you a memory." She wishes for wealth and forgets her first apartment. She wishes for health and forgets an old heartbreak. She thinks a long time about the third, then says, "I wish I knew what was worth remembering." The genie hands the lamp back and says, "That one's free. It's the wish everyone means."
56. A cop pulls over a man driving a truck full of penguins. "Take these penguins to the zoo right now." Next day, same truck, same penguins, all wearing sunglasses. "I told you to take them to the zoo!" The driver says, "I did. Today we're going to the beach — and officer, before you ask, yes, they pooled their fish."
57. An old couple sues each other for divorce at ninety-five. The judge asks, "Why now, after seventy years?" The wife says, "We were waiting for the children to die."
58. A man walks into a psychiatrist's office wearing nothing but plastic wrap. The psychiatrist looks him up and down and says, "Well, I can clearly see you're nuts." The man says, "Actually I'm here about my brother." The psychiatrist says, "Then why the plastic wrap?" The man says, "He wraps everything. I wanted you to feel the urgency."
59. Two antennas meet on a roof, fall in love, and get married. The ceremony was nothing special, but the reception was incredible — until the toaster in apartment 4B started picking up the vows and the whole building learned what the groom promised about "no more dish."
60. A man tells his priest, "Father, I'm 92 and last night I made love to two 25-year-olds. Twice." The priest asks, "When did you last confess?" The man says, "Never — I'm Jewish." "Then why are you telling me?" The man beams: "Father, I'm telling everyone."
61. A dragon applies for a job at a candle factory. "Experience?" asks the manager. The dragon exhales gently and lights four hundred wicks in perfect unison. "You're hired!" says the manager. "One question — why do you want this job?" The dragon looks at his feet. "My therapist says I need to stop burning villages and start lighting small, achievable fires."
62. A guy brags to his buddy, "My wife's an angel." His buddy sighs into his beer, "You're lucky. Mine's still alive."
63. Noah's neighbor watches him build the ark for forty years and finally asks, "What's it for?" Noah explains the flood, the animals, the divine plan. The neighbor laughs himself sick. Forty days later, treading water, the neighbor pounds on the hull: "NOAH! COME ON! WE WERE FRIENDS!" A porthole opens and Noah leans out: "Gary, I invited you to the boat-warming SEVEN TIMES. You said, quote, 'sounds like a cult.'"
64. A mime is arrested for disturbing the peace. At the station, they put him in a lineup. The witness studies the five men, then points at the mime and says, "That one — he's the only one still pretending to be behind glass."
65. A moth flies into a podiatrist's office. "Doc, my life is meaningless. My wife despises me, my son thinks I'm a coward, I sit in the dark contemplating the void." The podiatrist says, "You need a psychiatrist. Why did you come in here?" The moth says, "The light was on." The podiatrist writes him a referral, then quietly leaves the light on the next night too, and the night after that, because sometimes that's all the medicine anyone has.
66. Three vampires walk into a bar. The first orders a blood. The second orders a blood. The third orders a hot water. The bartender raises an eyebrow. The third vampire holds up a used tea bag with two tiny fang holes in it. "Found this in the alley. I'm not proud, but I am employed at a wellness startup and this is basically our whole business model."
67. A woman asks a psychic, "Will my husband ever stop gambling?" The psychic gazes into the crystal ball and says, "Ma'am, he bet me twenty bucks I'd say no."
68. A chicken walks into a library, says "Book," and receives a book. She returns it the next day: "Book, book." Two books. This continues until the librarian, suspicious, follows the chicken to the pond, where she hands the stack to a frog who examines each one and says, "Reddit, reddit, reddit."
69. A man is on trial for calling the mayor a pig. The judge fines him and, curious, the man asks, "So I can't say the mayor is a pig. Can I say a pig is the mayor?" The judge shrugs, "That's not illegal, no." The man turns to the gallery: "Ladies and gentlemen, the pig is the mayor."
70. God and Satan share a fence line in the afterlife, and Satan's side keeps flooding God's garden. God says, "Fix your drainage or I'll sue." Satan laughs so hard he cracks a hoof: "Oh yeah? Where are YOU gonna find a lawyer?"
71. A tourist in a small village asks an old man on a porch, "Lived here your whole life?" The old man spits thoughtfully into the yard and says, "Not yet."
72. A boy asks his grandfather, "Grandpa, can you make a sound like a frog?" Grandpa says, "Why, sweetheart?" The boy says, "Because Grandma says when you croak, we're all going to Disneyland."
73. A panda walks into a cafe, eats a sandwich, fires two shots into the ceiling, and heads for the door. "Why?!" screams the waiter. The panda tosses him a badly punctuated wildlife manual and says, "I'm a panda. Look it up." The entry reads: "PANDA. Large bear-like mammal. Eats, shoots and leaves." The waiter frames the page and doubles the menu prices, because that panda is now the most reliable customer he has: same order, every Tuesday, and he always pays for the ceiling.
74. A man's doctor tells him he has to stop pleasuring himself. "Why, doc? Is it my heart? My eyesight?" The doctor glances at the door. "No. It's just that I'm trying to examine you."
75. An angel and a demon are assigned to the same man. After forty years, they file a joint report requesting reassignment. The reason, in both their handwriting: "Subject does not require temptation or protection. Subject has a group chat."
76. A wealthy man on his deathbed makes his wife promise to bury his money with him. At the funeral, she places a small box in the casket. Her sister hisses, "You didn't actually—" The widow lifts her chin. "I'm a Christian woman. I keep my word. I wrote him a check."
77. A horse walks into a bar. The bartender says, "Why the long face?" The horse says, "My wife left me, I lost the farm, and this morning the vet used the word 'glue' in a sentence I didn't like." The bartender pours him a double, on the house, because some setups deserve to be treated like people.
78. Two goldfish are in a tank. One turns to the other and says, "You man the guns, I'll drive." The other says, "This is the fourth time this week, Dale. It's a fishbowl. We're goldfish. My memory resets every three seconds and even I'M tired of this bit."
79. A salesman knocks on a door and a ten-year-old answers holding a martini and a cigar. The salesman falters, "Uh... is your mother home?" The kid flicks his ash and says, "What do you think?"
80. A conductor is jailed for a crime of passion — he strangled a cellist who kept rushing the tempo. In prison, he starts a choir. At his parole hearing, the board asks if he's reformed. He says, "Completely. In here, when someone can't keep time, I have learned to simply close my eyes and think of Brahms." Parole granted. Two weeks later he's back. The warden doesn't even ask. The conductor sets down his baton and says, "Wedding band. Cumbia. The drummer had a click track and CHOSE not to use it."
81. A man walks into a pet store and asks for a parrot that doesn't talk. "This one," says the clerk, "raised by librarians." The man buys it. For a year, silence. Then one night the parrot says, "Your toast is burning." The man is astonished: "You can talk! Why haven't you?" The parrot shrugs, "Up to now, everything's been fine."
82. A genie emerges from a lamp in a nursing home rec room and offers the residents one collective wish. They confer for three hours, form a subcommittee, table two motions, and finally present their wish: "We'd like the thermostat left alone." The genie weeps, grants it, and retires on the spot, saying he has finally met people who understand the true nature of power.
83. A duck, a priest, and a horse walk into a bar. The bartender looks up and says, "What is this, some kind of joke?" The priest says, "Yes, and frankly the working conditions are terrible. The duck's been in four hundred of these, and the horse hasn't gotten a new line since the forties." The horse sighs. The bartender asks the horse, "Rough?" and the horse says, "Don't. Just don't."
84. A stockbroker dies and Saint Peter shows him two lines: one for people who lost other people's money, stretching past the horizon, and one for brokers who only lost their own, containing a single nervous man. The broker asks the man, "How'd you manage it?" The man says, "Oh, I'm not in line. I'm the exhibit."
85. A farmer's neighbor comes over and says, "I heard you've got a horse that does math." The farmer whistles and asks the horse, "What's seven minus seven?" The horse says nothing, does nothing. The neighbor scoffs, "That's not math." The farmer says, "That's the most advanced math there is. He knows when the answer's nothing, you keep your mouth shut. Took me sixty years."
86. Little Timmy's goldfish dies, and his dad organizes a lavish backyard funeral to teach him about grief. Casket, eulogy, tiny headstone. Afterward, Timmy tugs his sleeve. "Dad, that was such a nice funeral." The dad tears up, "Thanks, buddy." Timmy nods thoughtfully. "Can we kill the hamster next?"
87. A man walks into a tattoo parlor and asks for a tattoo of a hundred-dollar bill on his back. "Why?" asks the artist. "Three reasons. One, I like to sit on my money. Two, when I want to see my money, I just look in the mirror. Three, when my wife wants money, she can climb on my back and blow a hundred bucks."
88. A wolf in sheep's clothing infiltrates a flock, but the sheep figure it out immediately because he keeps making eye contact and asking follow-up questions. Instead of running, they elect him treasurer. When the farmer finally discovers him, the flock's savings have tripled and predator incidents are down ninety percent. "Aren't you going to eat them?" asks the farmer. The wolf looks up from a spreadsheet. "Eat my clients? What am I, a hedge fund?"
89. An old sailor tells a tourist, "See this peg leg? Shark. See this hook? Sword fight in Macao. See this eye patch?" The tourist leans in. "Seagull crapped in my eye." The tourist frowns. "You lost an eye to seagull droppings?" The sailor sighs. "Second day with the hook."
90. God is doing performance reviews. He calls in the platypus's designer. The angel shuffles in with his portfolio. God holds up the platypus. The angel says, "Sir, I can explain." God says, "It's venomous, it lays eggs, it sweats milk, and it locates food through electric fields." The angel braces for termination. God stamps the file: PROMOTED. "The committee designed the horse. YOU had a vision."
91. A husband and wife are playing golf. She hits a drive straight into a groundskeeper's shed, and the ball ricochets out and shatters their own windshield. The husband inspects the damage and says, "Honey, next time, aim for the shed."
92. A snake goes to the optometrist and gets glasses. A week later he's back, depressed. "Glasses not working?" asks the doctor. "They work great," says the snake. "Turns out I've been dating a garden hose for two years. And doc — she took the breakup better than I did."
93. A tightrope walker tells his doctor he can't sleep. "Anxiety?" asks the doctor. "No," says the tightrope walker. "My wife left me for the human cannonball." The doctor winces. "How do you handle that?" The tightrope walker stares out the window. "You don't understand. Every night I have to walk past him. And every night, that son of a bitch gets fired and I don't."
94. Three old men discuss how they'd like to go. First: "In my sleep, painless." Second: "Saving a child from a fire, heroic." Third: "Me? Shot by a jealous husband at ninety-five." The others nod respectfully, because dream big or don't dream at all.
95. A software engineer, a hardware engineer, and a project manager are driving down a mountain when the brakes fail. They wrestle the car to a stop. The PM says, "Let's convene a working group on brake failure." The hardware engineer says, "Let me take them apart and find the fault." The software engineer says, "First, let's push it back up and see if it happens again."
96. A guy asks the gym trainer, "Which machine should I use to impress women?" The trainer looks him over and points out the front window. "The ATM."
97. A missionary spends five years teaching a remote village English. On graduation day, his star pupil gives a speech: flawless grammar, perfect diction. Afterward the missionary asks, "Any questions about English?" The pupil raises a hand. "Yes. Why did you tell us 'ghoti' could spell 'fish' and then act surprised when nobody wanted to learn to read?" The missionary has no answer. To this day the village speaks perfect English and refuses, on principle, to write any of it down.
98. A woman's husband has been in a coma for months. The nurses notice that whenever they sponge-bathe him, his vitals improve. They suggest the wife try something more intimate. She goes in, closes the curtain. Flatline alarms. The staff burst in. She's standing there bewildered: "I don't understand. I just told him about my day."
99. A bartender says, "We don't serve time travelers in here." A time traveler walks into a bar.
100. A rooster and an owl get into an argument about who works harder. "I wake the whole farm," says the rooster. "I patrol all night," says the owl. They agree to swap jobs for a day. At 3 p.m. the farmer finds the owl passed out on the fence and the rooster in a tree, wide-eyed and vibrating. "Never again," says the rooster. The third returns empty-handed but memorizes the family's conversation and reports: "They come every Sunday. They always sit by the oak. The child drops one-third of everything." The colony builds a permanent outpost by the oak, feeds off it for a decade, and erects, in time, a statue not of the ant who carried the most, but of the one who listened — which the queen dedicates with the words, "Muscle fed us for a day. Gossip fed us for ten years."
288. A retired burglar gives a security seminar to wealthy homeowners. "Forget the alarms," he says, pacing. "You know what stopped me every time? Toys in the yard. Toys mean a dog or kids, and kids mean someone's always awake. A porch light burned out for months means nobody's watching — but a NEW bulb means somebody cares. And a doormat that says 'WELCOME' worn down in the middle?" He stops pacing. "That means people come and go all day, and one of them will see me." A hand goes up: "So what SHOULD we buy?" The burglar sighs. "Neighbors. You can't buy them. That was the whole seminar. The rich were always easiest, and now you know why. Coffee's in the back."
289. A parrot escapes and joins a flock of wild crows. Years later, birdwatchers report a murder of crows that has learned to say "WHO'S A GOOD BOY" before dive-bombing hawks, which researchers describe as "tactically devastating." The parrot, when finally photographed, has been given what appears to be a leadership position. His original owner saw the news story and drove out to the forest, calling his name. The whole flock descended, surrounded the man — and the parrot marched out of the ranks, looked up at him, and said, in the man's own voice, "We don't say his old name anymore." Then, softer, in his own: "But he can visit. Bring crackers. Enough for everyone. He has a family now."
290. A CEO institutes mandatory fun: trust falls, escape rooms, a company anthem. Morale drops. He hires a consultant who observes for a week, then delivers a one-line report: "They had fun before. You were just never invited, and now you've made yourself the theme." The CEO, to his eternal credit, read it twice, canceled the anthem, and simply started eating lunch in the cafeteria without an agenda. It took eight months before anyone sat with him, and the consultant's follow-up report was also one line: "Progress. Someone complained about you TO you. Do not fix it. That's the trust fall."
291. A fisherman's wife tires of his fish stories and installs a fish scale by the door, with a camera. First trip: he claims a twenty-pounder, weighs it — nine pounds, on camera. Instead of shame, something surprising happens: he becomes fascinated. He starts weighing everything, keeping logs, studying seasons. Within five years he's the most accurate fishing guide in the state, booked solid, quoted in magazines. At his retirement, his wife gives a toast: "I bought that scale to catch him lying." She raises her glass. "Turns out he never loved the lying. He loved the fish. The lies were just the biggest units of measurement he had before I bought him better ones."
292. Two gargoyles on opposite towers of a cathedral have gossiped across the roof for centuries, and one is finally selected for restoration and cleaning. He returns gleaming, pristine, all his edges crisp. Silence from the far tower. Days pass. Finally the cleaned gargoyle calls across, "Say something. It's still me." The weathered one replies, "Your left horn. They fixed the chip." "So?" "The chip was from the Peasants' Revolt. You DUCKED and I LAUGHED and you got clipped by a rock meant for the bishop, and now the only proof is my word." A long silence. Then: "Was the bishop worth saving?" "God, no." "Then tell it your way. You always did anyway." And that is why the tour guides now say the LEFT gargoyle saved the bishop, and the right one has never once corrected them, which if you know gargoyles is the purest love there is.
293. A man's doctor gives him six months to live. He doesn't tell anyone; instead he spends the six months fixing things quietly — the church roof, his brother's marriage (one arranged fishing trip), the pothole on Route 9 (he just... paved it, at night, nobody knows how). Month seven arrives; he doesn't die. Month eight. His doctor, mortified, admits the scans were switched: he's fine. The man sits in the parking lot a long time, then drives to the diner and orders pie, and when the waitress asks how he's doing, he says the sentence he then said every day for the next thirty years: "I'm on the bonus round, sweetheart, and today I'm thinking about the library steps."
294. A wizard's apprentice is terrible at magic but exceptional at paperwork. Spells fizzle, potions curdle — but his grimoire filing system is flawless, and he alone can find any incantation in under a minute. When the dark lord attacks, the kingdom's archmages fall one by one, spells failing under pressure, until the apprentice, unable to cast anything himself, simply stands behind the last exhausted archmage handing her exactly the right scroll at exactly the right moment, in sequence, for six straight hours, like a surgical nurse of the arcane. The kingdom names the victory after her. She spends the ceremony trying to hand him the medal, and the official history now includes her whole speech, which began: "Magic won the battle, and I will tell you where magic was FILED."
295. A talking dog finally tells his family why he's never spoken before: "You never asked a question and waited." They protest — they talk to him constantly! "You talk AT me," says the dog, not unkindly. "'Who's a good boy' is rhetorical. I've known the answer for eleven years." Chastened, they ask him a real question: what does he actually want? The dog thinks for a long time, lying by the fire, and finally says, "The vacuum. I want to watch you throw the vacuum in the river. I know it's not rational. You asked."
296. A pope and a plumber die the same day, and heaven's orientation pairs arrivals alphabetically. During the tour, the plumber keeps asking practical questions: "Who maintains the fountains? Where does the river of life drain? Is the water pressure this good everywhere?" The angel guide answers happily. The pope asks about seating arrangements near the throne. At assignment time, the plumber is given a workshop by the celestial waterworks, and the pope gets a lovely but ordinary residence. When he inquires — delicately — about the disparity, the angel checks the ledger and explains: "Your Holiness, we get a pope every few decades. But in all of recorded eternity, that man is the first to arrive already asking how to help."
297. A raccoon gets into a man's garage every night despite increasingly elaborate defenses: locks, lights, an alarm, chicken wire, a motion-activated sprinkler. Each morning, defenses defeated, one item rearranged, nothing taken. The man, now sleep-deprived and obsessed, sets up cameras and discovers the raccoon isn't eating anything — it's organizing. Nuts into jars. Tools grouped by size. Night after night. His garage is now the tidiest in the county. He gives up, installs a small door, and leaves out snacks. His wife tells the neighbors he "hired help." The raccoon, for its part, has started on the shed, and the man has begun leaving out small, respectful notes with requests, some of which get honored, and if you think either of them is going to acknowledge the arrangement directly, you don't know men or raccoons.
298. An opera singer loses her voice the week of her farewell performance. Doctors, steam, silence, prayer — nothing. The show must go on, so she stands on stage opening night and mouths the entire performance while, in the pit, hidden, her understudy of twenty years sings it. The crowd weeps; the critics rave; and at the final curtain, the diva does something unscripted: she walks into the pit, hauls the understudy up onto the stage by the wrist, and points at her, wordless, until the audience understands and comes up out of their seats roaring. Her voice came back the following month. She never used it professionally again. "I spent thirty years being heard," she told the one reporter she ever granted an interview. "The last thing it ever did was point. Best note I ever hit."

He rebrands as "artisanal, riddle-to-table, single-origin trolling," posts one blurry photo of the bridge at dawn, and is booked eight months out at forty dollars a crossing, mostly tech workers who describe being threatened by an authentic troll as "grounding." His one-star reviews all say the same thing — "he let my kids across free and only menaced ME" — and his response to each is identical: "Correct. That is the ancient way. Five stars from your children."
300. A hundred-year-old man is interviewed on his birthday. "To what do you attribute your longevity?" He holds up a finger: "Never argue." The reporter says, "Surely there's more to it — diet, exercise, genes?" The old man nods agreeably: "You know what, you're probably right."
