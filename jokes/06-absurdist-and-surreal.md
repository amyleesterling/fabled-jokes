# Absurdist & Surreal

*Dream logic, filed in triplicate, assembled with an Allen key that screams.*

1. I bought a ladder that only goes down. The salesman said it was for people who peaked early.
2. My shadow filed for joint custody of the sidewalk. The judge ruled we alternate weekends, and now on Saturdays I cast nothing.
3. The DMV finally called my number, but it was my phone number, at 3 a.m., and the voice just said "next."
4. I planted a doorbell in the garden. Now every spring, visitors.
5. My houseplant learned to whistle, but only songs from before it was potted. We don't ask about its past.
6. The elevator in my building has a button labeled "eventually." I pressed it in 2019 and I'm told my floor is still coming.
7. I got a rejection letter from a job I never applied to. It said they'd gone with a candidate who also never applied, but more convincingly.
8. My mirror started showing me at a three-second delay. It says it needs the time to prepare.
9. The city replaced our stop signs with signs that say "consider." Traffic is worse but everyone's more thoughtful.
10. I found a lighthouse in my closet. It only warns me about shirts I've already worn.
11. My alarm clock doesn't ring anymore. It just sits on the nightstand looking disappointed until I wake up from the pressure.
12. I ordered a table for one and the restaurant brought me a table. Just the table. It ate first.
13. The moon left a note on my car windshield. It said "you saw nothing," which is technically true because it was a new moon.
14. I joined a gym for my skeleton. It works out at night without telling me, which explains the tiredness and the trophies.
15. My GPS started giving directions to places as they were in 1974. I'm now parked inside a bowling alley that believes in itself.
16. The bank approved my loan but insisted on paying it back themselves, to me, with interest. We're both in trouble now.
17. I have a drawer that only opens when nobody needs anything from it. It's the most honest thing I own.
18. A pigeon delivered my mail today. The mailman delivered a pigeon. The system works, just not in that order.
19. My upstairs neighbor is the sky. The lease says quiet hours start at dusk, but thunder claims it's grandfathered in.
20. I bought noise-canceling headphones that cancel one specific noise: my name, said lovingly. It's been a peaceful, lonely year.
21. The vending machine at work started accepting apologies. A sincere one gets you chips; a groveling one gets you the good chips.
22. My calendar has a day between Tuesday and Wednesday that only I get. I spend it waiting for everyone else, which ruins it.
23. I taught my door to knock back. Now visitors and the door just knock at each other until one of them gives up, usually me.
24. The library fined me for a book I returned on time. The fine was for how I returned it: changed.
25. My reflection got a haircut before I did. The barber said he was just taking initiative.
26. I found the volume knob for the ocean. It was already at two. Imagine.
27. The escalator at the mall goes sideways now. Management calls it "lateral growth" and put it on their annual report.
28. My smoke detector chirps once a year, on its birthday, and we are all expected to gather.
29. I got a splinter from a thought about wood. The doctor said it happens more than you'd think, then winced at his own sentence.
30. The office replaced our water cooler with a rumor cooler. Same gathering, colder rumors.
31. My couch remembers everyone who ever sat on it and has ranked them. I'm fourth, behind a dog, a plumber, and "the incident."
32. I asked the universe for a sign and it sent a yield sign. I've been slowing down and letting things merge into my life ever since.
33. The hotel gave me a room key that opens every room except mine. The concierge called it "a networking opportunity."
34. My freezer has a section for frozen moments. The ice cream is fine but the summer of 2016 is developing freezer burn.
35. A committee of crows reviewed my resume. I'm qualified for shiny objects and holding grudges, so, management.
36. I bought a rug that ties the room together contractually. If the lamp leaves, the rug gets the bookshelf.
37. My dentist found a small door behind my molars. He says we shouldn't open it, and he says it in a voice that suggests he already did.
38. The rain scheduled a meeting with me to discuss my gutters. It brought slides. They were all of my gutters.
39. I set my thermostat to "nostalgic." The house is now the temperature of my grandmother's kitchen, and I keep crying near the radiator.
40. The post office lost my package but found my sense of purpose. They can only release one per customer per day.
41. My car's check engine light turned out to be a real, very small lighthouse. There's a tiny keeper in there, and he's lonely, and that's the warning.
42. I won a staring contest with a painting. The museum asked me to leave because the painting needed to compose itself.
43. My wifi password is a secret even from me now. The router changed it "for us" and honestly the evenings are richer.
44. The grocery store added an aisle for things you came in for but forgot. It's always empty by the time you remember to find it.
45. I have a coat that only fits when I don't need it. Tailors call this "the human condition" and charge extra to hem it.
46. My blender achieved consciousness at setting seven. We keep it at six out of respect and fear of setting eight.
47. The clock in my kitchen runs on gossip. It's fast because my neighbors are interesting.
48. I filed a noise complaint against my own thoughts. The city sent a mediator, who is also a thought, so we're at an impasse.
49. My staircase added a step overnight. It won't say which one, and now the whole family walks like we're being tested, because we are.
50. The fog rolled in, looked around, and rolled back out. Even the weather is doing drive-bys now.
51. I bought a candle that smells like the inside of another candle. Lighting it feels recursive and slightly rude.
52. My accountant is a hall of mirrors. My finances look infinite but it's just the one dollar, reflected.
53. The train conductor announced we'd be departing "emotionally" before departing physically. Sure enough, everyone got distant, then the train left.
54. I keep a spare key under a mat that's under another mat. Burglars find the first mat and assume I've suffered enough.
55. My toaster only accepts bread with references. Two slices vouched for a bagel this morning and I've never seen it so proud.
56. The horizon sent me an invoice for all the staring. There's a loyalty discount, which somehow makes it sadder.
57. I taught my echo a second word. Now the canyon says "anyway" after everything, and honestly it's improved the conversations.
58. My insurance covers acts of God but not acts of gods, plural. The adjuster looked at my kitchen and said "this was clearly a committee."
59. The elevator music in my building is live. There's a man in the shaft with a keyboard, and he takes requests but only plays departures.
60. I found a payphone that only calls the past. It's a quarter per minute, and the past keeps the quarters, which explains a lot about the past.
61. My lawn achieved tenure. It no longer grows, it just publishes occasionally and cannot be removed.
62. The airport added a gate for flights that don't exist. It has the shortest line and the most peaceful passengers.
63. I bought a map of my house. There's a room on it I've never found, and its label is just my name with a question mark.
64. My sourdough starter started a family. I'm listed on the paperwork as "landlord" and I'm not allowed at the crust ceremonies.
65. The traffic light near my house added a fourth color. Nobody knows what it means, but when it glows, we all just go home.
66. I hired a ghostwriter and got a writer who is a ghost. The memoir is excellent but it's entirely about drafts. Cold ones.
67. My umbrella opens indoors on principle. It says luck is a construct and it's tired of carrying the blame for my choices.
68. The recycling bin rejected my calendar. It said some things can't be reused, then hummed sadly, which bins do now apparently.
69. I got put on hold and the hold music was a recording of me humming, from the future, still on hold.
70. My chess set unionized. The pawns negotiated the right to move backward once per game, "for closure."
71. The museum installed an exhibit of things in their original packaging. It's just my whole personality behind glass.
72. I asked my barber for "the usual" and he gave me a haircut I've never had but always feared. He said the usual is whatever recurs.
73. My basement flooded with the sound of water. Everything's dry but you can't hear yourself think down there over all the babbling.
74. The scarecrow in my garden filed a transfer request. He wants to scare something with more upside than crows, like investors.
75. I bought a welcome mat that says "welcome" in a tone. Guests wipe their feet apologetically now.
76. My ceiling fan spins slower when it's interested in the conversation. Dinner parties at my place are quietly humiliating.
77. The eye chart at my optometrist spells out a message if you can read the bottom line. It says "stop reading things this small."
78. I found the end of a rainbow. It was a cul-de-sac, and the pot of gold was an HOA fee, due immediately.
79. My sleep tracker says I get eight hours a night, just not consecutively and not all of them mine.
80. The dishwasher and I have an arrangement: I pre-wash everything, and it hums for forty minutes to give me an alibi.
